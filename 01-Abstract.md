In housekeeping services, demand release, service matching, ordering, payment, and reviews require coordination among users, service staff, and the platform; offline or fragmented management often leads to delayed information, inconsistent service standards, unclear order circulation, and difficulty tracking complaints and refunds, which lowers supply-demand matching efficiency, weakens closed-loop control, and leaves operational statistics inconsistent. This study targets housekeeping workflows and designs a front-end/back-end separated housekeeping service management system. The front end uses Vue 3, Element Plus, and ECharts to build interactive functions for service display, recommendation and search, appointment ordering, review submission, browsing and favorites, and the personal center. The back end is built on Spring Boot 3.2, Spring Security, MyBatis-Plus, JWT, and MySQL to form a unified business layer. It covers modules such as users and roles, menu permissions, service categories, service items, service staff, order status and refunds, payment integration, reviews, banners, browsing history, favorites, statistical reports, file uploads, and data migration. By unifying permission control, transaction states, and audit data governance, the system improves closed-loop order management and operational analytics, supporting supply-demand matching and standardized operations, and enabling daily operations and service quality supervision in real service scenarios. Keywords: housekeeping service system; service orders; service staff; Spring Boot
